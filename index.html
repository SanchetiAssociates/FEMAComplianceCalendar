🌐 FDI Compliances — Inbound Foreign Investment

Applicable to Indian Companies, OPCs, LLPs receiving foreign investment. Governed by FEMA 20(R)/2017-RB & Non-Debt Instruments Rules, 2019.

Form / Filing Applicability Trigger / Due Date Filed With Frequency Remarks
Entity Master Form (EMF) — FIRMS Registration Indian CompanyLLP One-time: Before first FDI receipt (mandatory pre-registration) FIRMS portal (firms.rbi.org.in) — self-registration by entity One-Time Mandatory registration on FIRMS portal before any FDI can be received or reported. Entities without EMF cannot file FC-GPR or receive further investment. Without this, the entity is declared non-compliant.
⚠️ ARF — Advance Remittance Form Indian Company ABOLISHED w.e.f. 1 Sep 2018 N/A — Subsumed into FC-GPR under SMF Discontinued ARF has been merged with Form FC-GPR under the Single Master Form (SMF) w.e.f. 1 September 2018 vide FEMA 20(R)(3)/2018. Separate ARF filing is no longer required. Receipt of funds details are now reported within FC-GPR itself on FIRMS portal.
FC-GPR (Foreign Currency – Gross Provisional Return) Indian CompanyLLP Within 30 days of allotment of shares/debentures/warrants to foreign investor RBI via AD Category-I Bank on FIRMS portal Event-Based Covers equity, CCDs, CCPS, warrants. Valuation certificate from Registered Valuer / SEBI MB mandatory. Shares must be dematerialised (MCA Rule 9B for pvt cos.).
FC-TRS (Foreign Currency – Transfer of Shares) Indian CompanyResident IndividualNRI / OCI Within 60 days of receipt/remittance of funds or execution of transfer deed, whichever is earlier AD Category-I Bank → RBI via FIRMS Event-Based Applies to sale, gift or swap of equity instruments between resident and non-resident. Applicable for both directions. Not for NRI–to–NRI or R–to–R transfers.
LLP-I (Foreign Investment in LLP) LLP Within 30 days of receipt of capital contribution by foreign partner AD Bank → RBI via FIRMS Event-Based Capital contribution or profit share for foreign partners.
LLP-II (Divestment/Transfer in LLP) LLP Within 60 days of transfer of capital contribution AD Bank → RBI via FIRMS Event-Based Divestment or secondary transfer by foreign partner.
CN — Convertible Note Indian Startup Company Within 60 days of issue or transfer AD Bank → RBI via FIRMS Event-Based Startup as defined in DPIIT notification. Min. investment: ₹25 lakh. Conversion within 5 years.
DRR — Depository Receipts Listed Indian Company Within 30 days of issue/transfer AD Bank → RBI via FIRMS Event-Based ADR/GDR issuance by listed companies.
ESOP (Employee Stock Options / Sweat Equity) Indian Company Within 30 days of grant/issue AD Bank → RBI via FIRMS Event-Based Issued to non-resident employees. Pricing: not less than FMV at grant date.
DI — Downstream Investment Indian Company with Foreign Ownership Within 30 days of downstream investment AD Bank → RBI via FIRMS Event-Based Indirect foreign investment (IFI). Total foreign ownership > 50% triggers full FDI treatment.
FLA Return (Foreign Liabilities & Assets) Indian CompanyLLPPartnership 15 July 2026 (for FY 2025-26) RBI directly via FLAIR portal (flair.rbi.org.in) Annual Mandatory even if no new FDI/ODI in the year but outstanding balances exist. DSC Class 3 required. Provisional filing: 15 July. Revision allowed till 30 Sep.
FC-1 Filing with ROC Indian Company Within 30 days of allotment (parallel with FC-GPR) MCA Portal (ROC) Event-Based MCA filing on receipt of foreign capital (Form FC-1 under Companies Act). Done alongside FEMA filings.
ℹ️
Valuation Note: Shares issued to non-residents must be at a price not less than Fair Market Value (FMV) computed per Rule 11UA of Income Tax Rules (for unlisted companies) or SEBI ICDR guidelines (for listed companies). Valuation report must not be older than 90 days at the time of allotment.
🏛️
Govt. Route FDI — FIF/NSWS Portal (SOP dated 04.05.2026): All FDI proposals requiring Government approval must be filed online through the Foreign Investment Facilitation (FIF) / NSWS Portal. DPIIT assigns proposals to the relevant Administrative Ministry/Department. No physical filing required. MHA security clearance required for: Broadcasting, Telecom, Space, Defence, Civil Aviation, and investments covered under Press Note 2 of 2026 dated 15.03.2026 (Land Border Countries — expanded scope under FEM (NDI) Amendment Rules, 2026 dated 01.05.2026).

✈️ ODI Compliances — Overseas Direct Investment

Governed by FEM (Overseas Investment) Regulations, 2022 [FEMA 400/2022-RB]. Applies to Indian companies, LLPs, and resident individuals investing abroad in JV/WOS.

Form / Filing Applicability Trigger / Due Date Filed With Frequency Remarks
Form ODI Part I Indian CompanyLLPResident Individual Prior to making overseas investment (or within 30 days for automatic route) Designated AD Category-I Bank Event-Based UIN (Unique Identification Number) obtained. Captures investment details in JV/WOS abroad.
Share Certificate / Investment Evidence Indian CompanyResident Individual Within 30 days of receipt of share certificate / investment evidence from abroad Designated AD Bank Event-Based Documentary evidence of overseas investment must be submitted to AD immediately on receipt.
APR — Annual Performance Report (Form ODI Part II) Indian CompanyLLPResident Individual 31 December of each year AD Category-I Bank Annual For each JV/WOS. Based on audited accounts of the overseas entity. Delay blocks future ODI approvals. Must be filed even if no new investment made.
FLA Return (ODI portion) Indian CompanyLLP 15 July 2026 RBI via FLAIR portal Annual Same FLA return covers both FDI received and ODI made. Reports foreign assets held abroad as of 31 March.
Disinvestment / Winding Up Reporting Indian CompanyIndividual Within 30 days of disinvestment / winding up proceeds received AD Bank → RBI Event-Based Proceeds must be repatriated to India within 60 days of receipt by the overseas entity.
ODI in Financial Services Sector NBFC / Financial Entity Prior RBI approval required + post-investment reporting RBI (FEMA Department) Govt / RBI Approval Financial sector entities require RBI nod beyond automatic route. Requires NOC from sectoral regulator.
⚠️
APR Non-Filing Consequence: Failure to file APR before 31 December blocks future overseas investment under automatic route. The AD Bank is required to report non-filers to RBI. Compounding penalty is levied based on amount involved and delay period.

💰 External Commercial Borrowings (ECB) Compliances

Governed by FEM (Borrowing & Lending) Regulations, 2018 [FEMA 3(R)/2018-RB] and RBI Master Direction on ECB. Covers foreign currency and INR-denominated borrowings from non-resident lenders.

Form / Filing Applicability Trigger / Due Date Filed With Frequency Remarks
Loan Registration Number (LRN) — Form ECB Indian BorrowerAD Bank Before first drawdown RBI via AD Category-I Bank (CIMS Portal) Event-Based LRN is mandatory before any ECB drawdown. Delays block utilisation. Covers loans, bonds, FCCBs, RDBs, trade credits > 3 years.
ECB-2 Return (Monthly Reporting) All ECB Borrowers 7th of every month (for previous month) AD Category-I Bank → RBI (CIMS) Monthly Reports drawdowns, repayments, outstanding amount, interest paid, hedging positions. Any change in ECB terms also reported here.
ECB Agreement / Loan Modification Reporting Indian Borrower Within 7 days of change in terms (interest rate, maturity, lender change) AD Bank → RBI via CIMS Event-Based Any change in principal amount, lender, currency, end-use must be reported immediately.
Annual Hedging Compliance Statement Mandatory Hedging Entities Within 3 months of end of financial year AD Bank Annual For borrowers in infrastructure, core investment companies where mandatory hedging of 70% of ECB applies.
Trade Credit Reporting ImportersAD Bank Within 30 days of trade credit arrangement AD Bank Event-Based Short-term (up to 3 years) buyer's/supplier's credit for imports. AD Bank can approve up to USD 20 million per import transaction. Trade credit > USD 20 mn per transaction requires RBI approval. Advance import payment > USD 200,000 to non-MSE overseas suppliers requires bank guarantee / LC from AD Bank (may be waived on merits for established relations).

📦 Export & Import Compliances

FEMA 23(R)/2026-RB (effective 1 Oct 2026) replaces the 2015 Regulations. Introduces unified EDF replacing SOFTEX for services. Until 1 Oct 2026, existing framework continues.

📢
Major 2025 Change: Effective November 13, 2025, export proceed realisation period extended from 9 months → 15 months for goods; 18 months for INR-invoiced/settled exports. Advance payment shipment period extended from 1 year → 3 years. AD Banks can now grant extension without RBI permission in most cases.

🤝 Compounding of Offences (Section 15 of FEMA)
AuthorityJurisdictionCompounding FeeRemarks
Reserve Bank of India (RBI) All FEMA contraventions — compounding within 180 days of receipt of application (Section 15, FEMA 1999) Based on transaction amount and delay. Formula-based as per Master Direction on Compounding. Minimum ₹5,000; typically much higher based on amount and days Voluntary disclosure before ED notice significantly reduces fee. Application via AD Bank to RBI regional office. RBI / officers of Directorate of Enforcement authorised by Central Govt.

Appeals Hierarchy under FEMA Adjudication order → Section 17: Special Director (Appeals) → Section 19: Appellate Tribunal → Section 35: High Court As per order appealed against Note: FEMA does NOT have an "Appellate Tribunal for Foreign Exchange (ATFE)" — that term was under FERA. Under FEMA, the appellate body is simply the "Appellate Tribunal" under Section 18. Further appeal lies to High Court under Section 35 (not Supreme Court directly).

Version 2 — Corrections Applied (Cross-checked against FEMA Bare Act, SOP dated 04.05.2026, FEMA 20(R)/2017-RB, and RBI Master Directions):

5. LRS bank reporting corrected — AD Banks must report BOTH monthly (by 5th) AND daily T+1 transaction-wise data via XBRL, not just monthly.
6. Compounding period confirmed — 180 days from receipt of application per Section 15, FEMA 1999.
7. Press Note 2 of 2026 & FEM NDI Amendment Rules 2026 added — Land Border Country investment rules expanded w.e.f. 01.05.2026 from SOP dated 04.05.2026.
8. Govt. route FDI portal added — FIF/NSWS portal mandatory for Govt. approval route proposals (confirmed from SOP).
